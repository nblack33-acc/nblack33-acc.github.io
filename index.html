Programmable MIDI loopback instrument

Introduction

Hello! Today we'll be building a little MIDI player. It has three buttons;
  • One that starts & stops recording
  • One to select the note - pressing it will repeatedly increase the pitch, cycling through from middle C to B
  • One to move to the next note, and (when recording is finished) to play the song.

It also makes use of two other components;
  • A piezo speaker for sound output
  • An LED to indicate when recording mode is active

You can follow along with the instructions below, or view the full playlist here.

Part 1: Installing the buttons

  1. The buttons can be placed anywhere in the center of the breadboard, but should be next to each other
  2. Either terminal can be used as the ground terminal, but for simplicity, it should be kept consistent between buttons
  3. It is recommended to make the wires for the GPIO pins different, but similar colors, so that they are easy to recognize as more wires are added
